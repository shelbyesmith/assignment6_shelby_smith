Introduction

This data explores female lizard length of the Desert Grassland and Colorado Checkers lizards species. We will use a two-sample t-test to determine if there are significant differences in total length (mm) from the sample population collected in the Jornada Basin LTER Site from 1989 to 2006, published in David Lightfoots Lizard pitfall trap data (LTER-II, LTER-III) ver 36 .

Summary

A two sample t.test reveals that there is a significant difference in mean lengths between the female Desert Grassland lizard (CNUN) and the Colorado Checkered lizard (CNTE) at the Jornada Basin LTER Site. (Welchs two-sample t-test: t(46.43) = 9.28, p < 0.001), and the effect size is large (Cohens d = 2.42). The absolute difference in mean length between the female lizards species is 97 g (a 49.58% difference).

Figure 1: Mono Lake water levels from 1850 to 2017. The blue line shows the lake level measured in feet above sea level, with two specific points highlighted to reflect significant events in the lakes history. Horizontal dashed lines represent ecologically relevant lake levels. Water levels in the lake control salinity: high lake levels dilute the salt content, lowering salinity. High salinity, occurring at low water, decreases food availability for migrating and nesting birds. Data Source: Mono Lake Committee
